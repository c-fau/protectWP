The Site

The proposal is for a 24 MW (Mega Watt) solar farm as well as the supporting infrastructure, of which a 15MW lithium ion battery array is included. The proposed location is 3 fields (roughly 52 acres of farmland) 1 mile north of Hertford between Bengeo and Ware.

The site is accessible via 2 roads, one from the direction of Ware and the other from Bengo. This means that both areas will see an uplift in industrial traffic during and after the construction of the solar farm.

In addition to this, the access from Ware goes past a Primary and Secondary school, increasing traffic during and surrounding school hours.

Wildlife In The Area

The proposed area is home to many different and protected species! Bats are very present in the area, as are badgers and muntjac deer. Red Kites can always be seen in the skies, and even Lesser seen species such as Fallow deer have also been spotted on the fields of the proposed site.

As you can imagine the fences detailed in the screening documents would pose a major obstruction to the wildlife blocking their routes and forcing them away from the area.

The impact to bats and kites cannot be ignored, bats often mistake large flat surfaces for sources of water, this causes the bats to fly into the panels and sadly die. And kites having their hunting ground obstructed with huge amounts of cover cannot be overlooked.
